📌 Our key messaging themes

Blockchain Transparency as a Regulatory Asset

Public blockchains provide unique transparency — immutable records, real-time visibility, permanent audit trails — that should be leveraged as a core compliance capability, not treated as a burden.

CFTCOCCAMLA CDDAMLA BR
"Every on-chain transaction creates a permanent, publicly auditable record" — CFTC Response

Blockchain Analytics as Mandatory Compliance Infrastructure

Blockchain analytics should be required — not optional — across all regulated entities as a baseline compliance capability, with specific requirements for real-time monitoring and cross-chain visibility.

OCCAMLA CDDAMLA BR
"Require blockchain analytics as a compliance capability for all PPSIs" — OCC Response

Technology Neutrality with Quality Standards

Regulations should remain technology-neutral on implementation while establishing clear, objective quality and reliability standards. We advocate for a tiered data quality framework distinguishing forensic-grade outputs from heuristic-based analysis.

OCCAMLA CDDAMLA BR
"The RTS need not prescribe specific technologies... they should clarify quality expectations" — AMLA CDD Response

Regulatory Harmonisation Across Jurisdictions

Regulators should establish consistent, harmonised blockchain analytics standards across agencies and jurisdictions to prevent divergent interpretations and create a unified compliance baseline.

OCCAMLA CDD
"Coordinate with FDIC and Federal Reserve to develop harmonized blockchain analytics requirements" — OCC Response
